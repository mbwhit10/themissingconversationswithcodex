The Week I Ran Out of Answers

It doesn't take a dramatic event to realize your parenting playbook is out of date. Sometimes, it's a series of stacked moments that reveal the gaps. In one week my daughter argued—logically—for Snapchat to navigate her social world, I testified about AI in schools, and both my kids spent hours hiding in lockdown, terrified death was imminent. Those stacked moments made it obvious: the conversations our kids need today aren’t in any parenting playbook we inherited. They touch on safety, identity, and their future as they navigate a world that is nearly unrecognizable to the one from our own childhoods. For each one, I felt profoundly underprepared and didn't have time to become an expert before engaging.

“The conversations we need are largely missing—either because we don’t know they’re needed, or we simply don’t know what to say. How can any parent be expected to be an expert on all of this?”

Unfortunately, the institutions designed to support our children are impossibly behind. It's on us. If we, as parents, opt out of these essential conversations—either because we feel overwhelmed or a mistaken belief that someone else will step in—our kids will be adrift in a sea of powerful forces. That’s why The Missing Conversations exists—to help you build a compass rooted in your family’s throughlines and a map for the modern terrain so you can lean in with confidence to guide your children even when that feels impossibly hard.

Your Family's Compass: Building Resilience & Responding to the Urgent.

At the heart of "The Missing Conversations" is a powerful two-pronged approach designed to equip your family for any challenge, known or unknown. We combine enduring principles for long-term strength with practical, timely tools for immediate needs.

1. The Foundation: Family Throughlines

Just like good nutrition, exercise, and sleep build baseline physical health, your family's unique, enduring values and wisdom form a "Throughline"—a core resilience that prepares your kids for any future challenge. We help you uncover, articulate, and intentionally apply these timeless principles to everything from AI ethics to social pressures. This is about discovering and utilizing the strength you already have, creating an inner compass that guides your family.

2. The Practical Tools: The Conversation First Aid Kit

When specific, urgent challenges arise—a new app, a deepfake scare, a complex social dilemma—you need more than just general wisdom. Our "First Aid Kit" provides rapid-response guides to help you address these acute issues effectively. Each guide offers a "Lowdown," "Parent/Kid Perspectives," actionable "Conversation Starters," and a "Family Playbook" for strategies you can implement right away.

Our Guiding Principles for Every Conversation

  • Start with a Shared Reality: agree on what's really happening before you try to solve it.
  • Find the "And": frame every talk as you and your kid vs. the problem, not you vs. them.
  • Lead with Curiosity: your wisdom is the asset; begin with questions before authority.
  • Speak the Tension: name the pull you both feel without judgment so the real issue is on the table.
  • Make Space for Silence: let pauses breathe; big conversations rarely finish in one sitting.
